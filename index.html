Rocky Mountain National Park

Growing up, I visited Rocky Mountain National Park several times with my family. Oddly enough, I haven't been back as an adult, even though it's the closest park to home, however I do have good memories of exploring the park with my family and staying firmly on the ground with my parents while my brother climbed up the top of the waterfall.

Yellowstone National Park

My family and I stayed at Yellowstone for a week over the 4th of July one summer. I was probably around twelve at the time but I have really clear memories of this park--the stunning colors of some of the hot springs and the geysers like Old Faithful are really hard to forget. I really want to go back there soon.

Grand Canyon National Park

My family stopped at the Grand Canyon on the way to visit my grandfather in San Francisco. I'd like to go back and see it again.

Great Sand Dunes National Park

I visited this park in 2013 with my boyfriend, Patrick. It was the first national park we visited together and got us really interested in visiting more of them. Now we try to visit at least one or two a year. I'd love to go back to the sand dunes again--they were amazing!

Mesa Verde National Park

We visited Mesa Verde on the same trip as the Great Sand Dunes. This is a really interesting and unique park since the main feature is man-made instead of environmental. It's definitely worth a visit.

Arches National Park

I first visited Arches in May of 2014 during a trip to Moab, Utah. It was amazing and is still my favorite park. My favorite hike is Devil's Garden--it's around 7 miles total and you get to see 6 or 7 arches during the hike, but what I love most are the views. You get to climb up giant sandstone boulders and end up with amazing views of the park. We visited Arches again a couple of years later and will definitely go back in the future.

Canyonlands National Park

We visited Canyonlands on the same trip to Moab. It is a huge park, and the first time we went we didn't get a chance to explore much of it, so what we saw was kind of underwhelming. The second time we visited Moab, however, we spent much more time in Canyonlands, specifically in the "Islands in the Sky" section, and it was incredible.

Badlands National Park

We drove up to South Dakota to Badlands in 2015. To be honest, I found the park kind of disappointing. It was definitely a unique environment but kind of underwhelming compared to the parks in Utah.

Wind Cave National Park

Wind Cave is another park in South Dakota. We didn't know about it before our trip but found out about when we were already in South Dakota. We opted to skip our second day at Badlands and visit Wind Cave instead. It's a large cave system so we didn't get to explore much of it in just one day but it was great, and I'd really like to go back and see this one again if I'm ever in South Dakota.

Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park

This was a park I hadn't heard of until recently. It's in western Colorado near Montrose. We visited it in the summer in 2017 and it was beautiful! My favorite park was that it was not crowded at all. On one long hike we did we saw only one person and it was a park ranger.

Zion National Park

Zion is probably the most beautiful of all of the parks I've been to, but it was also the most crowded. I loved the park, but the hikes were so packed that it was at times hard to enjoy them.

Bryce Canyon National Park

Bryce Canyon was a really cool park as well-- we visited it on the same trip as Zion. It wasn't as pretty as Zion but it was much less crowded and had great hiking trails.
